## 1. WHAT ARE COOKIES?

A cookie is a small file stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) that contains information about your browsing habits. Cookies are identified by name and can be accessed by the server that created them.

Cookies serve several purposes:
- **Authentication:** Keeping you logged in
- **Personalization:** Remembering your preferences
- **Analytics:** Understanding how you use our Service
- **Functionality:** Ensuring features work correctly
- **Advertising:** Showing you relevant content

---

## 2. TYPES OF COOKIES WE USE

### 2.1 Essential/Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are required for the Service to function. They enable:
- User authentication and login
- Account security
- CSRF (Cross-Site Request Forgery) protection
- Basic functionality
- Session management

**Examples:**
- `session_id` - Maintains your login session
- `csrf_token` - Prevents unauthorized requests
- `auth_token` - Authenticates your identity
- `user_preferences` - Your basic settings

**Retention:** Duration of your session (deleted when you log out)
**Can be disabled:** NO - Service won't work without these

### 2.2 Functional Cookies

These cookies enhance your experience by remembering choices:
- Language preferences
- Dark/light theme preference
- Sidebar collapsed/expanded state
- Recently viewed items
- Page layout preferences

**Examples:**
- `theme_preference` - Dark or light mode
- `language` - Your preferred language
- `sidebar_state` - Collapsed or expanded
- `ui_preferences` - Layout and display options

**Retention:** 1 year (or until cleared)
**Can be disabled:** YES (via Cookie Settings)

### 2.3 Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how you use the Service:
- Pages you visit
- Features you use
- Time spent on pages
- Errors or issues encountered
- User flow and navigation patterns

**Examples:**
- `_ga` - Google Analytics
- `_gid` - Google Analytics session
- `mixpanel_distinct_id` - Mixpanel analytics
- `sentry_dsn` - Error tracking (no personal data)

**Retention:** 13-24 months (depends on provider)
**Can be disabled:** YES (via Cookie Settings)

### 2.4 Marketing/Advertising Cookies

These cookies track advertising effectiveness and enable retargeting:
- Campaign performance
- Ad impressions and clicks
- Retargeting (showing ads across other websites)
- Email campaign tracking
- Conversion tracking

**Examples:**
- `utm_source` - Traffic source
- `utm_campaign` - Campaign name
- `fbp` - Facebook Pixel
- `tracking_id` - Advertising tracking

**Retention:** 30-90 days
**Can be disabled:** YES (via Cookie Settings or consent banner)

## 4. HOW TO MANAGE COOKIES

### 4.1 Browser Settings

Most browsers allow you to:
- Accept or reject all cookies
- Accept only certain types of cookies
- Clear cookies when you close the browser
- Receive notifications when cookies are set
- Block third-party cookies

**Browser Instructions:**

**Chrome:** Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies

**Firefox:** Preferences → Privacy & Security → Cookies

**Safari:** Preferences → Privacy → Cookies

**Edge:** Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies

**Internet Explorer:** Tools → Internet Options → General tab (Cookies)

### 4.2 Our Cookie Settings Page

Visit: https://promptquill.dev/cookie-settings

Here you can:
- Choose which cookie categories to accept
- Disable non-essential cookies
- Update preferences anytime
- Download cookie report

### 4.3 "Do Not Track" Signal

If you enable "Do Not Track" in your browser:
- We honor DNT requests for analytics
- Functional cookies still operate (for Service to work)
- Marketing cookies are disabled
- Some features may not work optimally

## 6. LOCAL STORAGE AND SIMILAR TECHNOLOGIES

Beyond cookies, we also use:

### LocalStorage
- Persistent data stored on your device
- Survives browser closure
- Examples: user preferences, draft content
- Can be cleared from browser settings

### SessionStorage
- Data stored only during your session
- Cleared when you close the browser
- Examples: temporary form data

### Web Beacons
- Tiny transparent images embedded in pages/emails
- Track if you opened an email or visited a page
- Used for analytics

### UTM Parameters
- Special URL parameters (utm_source, utm_campaign)
- Track traffic source
- Stored in analytics systems

## 11. FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

**Q: Will my Internet connection be slower because of cookies?**
A: No. Cookies are tiny files (typically 4-8KB) and have negligible impact on speed.

**Q: Are cookies safe?**
A: Cookies themselves are safe and cannot execute viruses. However, always keep your browser updated.

**Q: Can cookies access files on my computer?**
A: No. Cookies only access information explicitly stored in them, not your files or programs.

**Q: How many cookies can I have?**
A: Most browsers support thousands. We use minimal cookies for optimal performance.

**Q: Can I disable essential cookies?**
A: You can, but the Service won't work properly without them.

**Q: How do I know which site set a cookie?**
A: Check your browser's cookie settings. Each cookie shows the domain that created it.

**Q: Can websites see cookies from other sites?**
A: No. A website can only access cookies it created (first-party cookies).
